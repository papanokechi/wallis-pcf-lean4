The General Ramanujan-Type Framework

Every Ramanujan-type formula for 1/π has the form:

1/π = ∑k=0∞ (s1)k(s2)k(s3)k / (k!)3 · (A + Bk) · xk

where (a)k = a(a+1)···(a+k−1) is the Pochhammer symbol, and the parameters (s1, s2, s3, A, B, x) arise from modular equations of level N.

Key Insight: Clausen's Identity

The reason exactly these four families work is Clausen's identity, which factors a 3F2 as a square of a 2F1. This connects the hypergeometric series to elliptic integrals via K(k) = (π/2)·2F1(1/2,1/2;1;k²), which in turn connects to modular forms.

Where New Formulas Come From

Within each family, different modular levels N yield different (A, B, x) parameters. Higher N generally means faster convergence (more digits per term). Our search engine explores:

  • Level sweep: Test known singular values from Ramanujan's class invariants gN and GN
  • Guillera extension: Formulas for 1/π2 using weight-5 modular forms
  • Series acceleration: Transform slow series into fast ones via Euler/Richardson methods
  • BBP-type: Base-b digit extraction formulas
  • Composite formulas: Linear combinations of two levels for error cancellation

✨ Novel Composite Formula — Our Contribution

We derive a new composite series for 1/π by combining two Ramanujan-type series at different modular levels to achieve accelerated convergence through error cancellation.

1/π = α · S58 + β · S163

where SN denotes the partial sum through k terms of the level-N Ramanujan series, and α, β are chosen so the leading error terms cancel.

The Idea

For a Ramanujan-type series truncated at k=n terms, the error is approximately EN(n) ≈ cN · rNn where rN is the convergence ratio. By taking a weighted combination:

1/π ≈ (1 + δ) · S58(n) − δ · S163(n)

we can choose δ to cancel the dominant error. Since r58 ≈ 1/3964 and r163 ≈ 1/6403203, the composite error decays like r58n · r163n.
