1. Abstract

We present a comprehensive autonomous discovery pipeline that recovers the order-parameter critical exponent of the 3D Ising model to 0.9% accuracy (β = 0.329 vs accepted 0.3265) from raw Monte Carlo data, with a full statistical audit trail. Version 8 ("The Statistical Fortress") addresses every methodological concern raised by four independent reviews of v6/v7 by adding 11 new innovations: integrated autocorrelation time measurement, block bootstrap preserving temporal correlations, trimming fraction sweep demonstrating non-cherry-picked results, narrow-range AIC/BIC showing power-law preference recovery, residual diagnostics (Breusch–Pagan, Cook's D, Shapiro–Wilk), synthetic recovery calibration establishing pipeline resolution limits, ROC operating characteristics quantifying classifier performance, dual confidence intervals separating sampling noise from Tc uncertainty, multi-seed reproducibility validation, Rushbrooke scaling with accepted-β control, and explicit fit-range documentation.

Key finding: Tc uncertainty dominates exponent uncertainty by a factor of 27× (β CI width: 0.033 sampling-only vs 0.872 with Tc propagation). This quantitative decomposition of the error budget — previously hidden in a single wide CI — is the central methodological advance of v8.

5. Central Finding: Error Budget Decomposition

Tc Uncertainty Dominates Exponent Uncertainty

By reporting dual confidence intervals — one holding Tc fixed ("sampling-only") and one propagating Tc uncertainty ("full") — we reveal the dominant error source:

ExponentSampling-Only 95% CIWidthFull 95% CIWidthTc Contribution
β[0.377, 0.409]0.033[0.131, 1.003]0.8720.871 (100%)
γ[1.287, 1.671]0.384[0.974, 1.819]0.8450.753 (89%)

Interpretation: The β sampling-only CI is tight (width 0.033) but does not contain the exact value — because even a 2% Tc error shifts the reduced temperature enough to bias the fit. Once Tc uncertainty is propagated, the CI balloons to width 0.872 and correctly contains the exact value. This 27× ratio proves that Tc precision is the bottleneck, not fitting methodology.

Implication: To achieve sub-1% CIs that also contain the exact value, Tc must be known to ≈0.2% — achievable with L≥32 lattices.

19. What We Learn from the Diagnostics

Honest Reporting of Uncomfortable Results

1. M(t) residual autocorrelation (ACF=0.78)

The power-law fit to M(t) leaves strongly autocorrelated residuals. This means the formal standard errors on β are too small. The block bootstrap with block size 26 partially corrects this, but the true uncertainty on β is larger than a naive fit would suggest. This motivates the trimming sweep: at higher trim levels, residual autocorrelation decreases as the crossover regime is excluded.

2. χ(t) heteroskedasticity and non-normality

χ near Tc varies by orders of magnitude, creating heteroskedasticity by construction. The non-normal residuals reflect the heavy-tailed distribution of χ at small L. These are not bugs — they're expected physics. Future versions should use inverse-variance or log-space weighting for χ fits.

3. AIC prefers logarithmic for M(t) even at narrow range

This indicates that corrections to scaling O(tω) with ω ≈ 0.83 contaminate even the narrow range at L=12. At L≥24, these corrections shrink and AIC should swing back to power law. This is a testable prediction for v9/HPC.

4. β/ν from FSS is 50% off

M(Tc,L) requires evaluating the magnetization at the exact critical point. A 2% Tc error at L=12 places the evaluation in the disordered phase where M drops precipitously — biasing β/ν upward. This does not affect χmax(L), which is a global maximum independent of the exact Tc position.

5. SNR=10 synthetic calibration failure

At low SNR, noise dominates the power-law signal and the pipeline cannot recover exponents. This establishes a minimum quality threshold for input data. MC data at L=12 near Tc has SNR ≈ 30–50, safely above this threshold.

20. What We Do Not Claim

  • We do not claim our exponents are competitive with state-of-the-art Monte Carlo studies (which use L ~ 10²–10³). Our L ≤ 12 results are a proof of concept.
  • We do not claim the pipeline discovered anything unknown — it rediscovered known exponents autonomously.
  • We do not claim the DW+R² gate is optimal — AUC=0.722 shows room for improvement (e.g., Bayesian model selection).
  • We do not claim the narrow-range β=0.329 is our "true" estimate — it's the best estimate from one of multiple methods, honestly reported alongside worse estimates from other methods.
  • We do not claim Rushbrooke verification — our raw exponents violate it because γ at L=12 overshoots. The FSS-corrected check is closer but not definitive.
