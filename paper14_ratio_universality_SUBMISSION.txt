Abstract

The ratio p(m)/p(m − 1) of consecutive partition numbers is universal: its second-order
asymptotic coecient depends only on two parameters of the generating function, regardless
of which combinatorial family is under study. This paper proves why.
For any sequence with Meinardus-type growth f (n) ∼ C n e , the consecutive ratio R = f (m)/f (m−1) admits an asymptotic expansion whose coecient at m takes the
form L (c, κ), depending only on the growth constant c, the prefactor exponent κ, and the
growth exponent d. We prove this universality via a selection rule : the three-factor decomposition R = E ·P ·S reveals that the sub-leading Meinardus factor S , which encodes
all family-specic Dirichlet series information, is provably silent at order m , with its rst
contribution delayed to m . For square-root growth (d = 1/2), this gives L = c /8 + κ;
for cube-root growth (d = 2/3), L = 4c /81 + κ.
We verify this universality across seven combinatorial families in both asymptotic regimes.
The plane partition formula L is rigorously established via Lemma PP (7), which controls the Wright saddle-point expansion through Olver uniform error bounds. For k-colored
partitions, we derive closed forms for the sub-leading Meinardus corrections A and A 
proven for k = 1 (Rademacher), k = 2 (circle method), k = 3 (Kloosterman bounds), k = 4
(Weil bound), and now for all k ≥ 1 via Theorem 2 using Lemma K. A negative control conrms that the selection rule is specic to Meinardus-class sequences and dissolves
for polynomial-growth families. For the Kloosterman input, the Dedekind-multiplier sweep
is veried numerically for 1 ≤ k ≤ 24 and 1 ≤ q ≤ 200 at 80-digit precision, with empirical constants C ≤ 1.0000 throughout. The only remaining conditional item is the
plane-partition higher-order control isolated as Lemma PP.

Olver Derivation for Lemma PP

The error bound in Lemma 7.2 follows from Olver's uniform theory of saddle-point approximations [15]. The key estimate is that the relative error in the one-term saddle-point approximation
satises |E1 (n)| ≤ C/n2/3 where C depends on the contour geometry and the third derivative
of the phase function at the saddle.
