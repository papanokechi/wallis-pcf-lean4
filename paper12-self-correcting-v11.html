Abstract

We present a pre-registered, fully autonomous pipeline for critical exponent discovery that not only estimates and validates its results, but diagnoses its own failure modes and automatically repairs the dominant systematic. Applied to the three-dimensional Ising model on modest lattices (L ≤ 12), the pipeline discovers Tc, fits power laws, and runs a comprehensive statistical audit without human intervention.

The central finding is an error budget decomposition via dual confidence intervals: Tc uncertainty inflates the β confidence interval by 27× over sampling noise alone (sampling-only 95% CI: [0.377, 0.409], width 0.033; full CI with Tc propagation: [0.131, 1.003], width 0.872), due to nonlinear propagation of Tc into reduced temperatures. The M(t) signal-to-noise ratio (SNR ≈ 31) maps to the synthetic calibration regime predicting ~19% error; χ(t) SNR ≈ 2 falls outside all calibrated ranges, explaining the persistent 23% γ error independently of fitting methodology.

The breakthrough contribution of this version is closed-loop self-repair: the self-diagnosis engine detects SEVERE Tc inflation, autonomously triggers single-histogram reweighting (Ferrenberg–Swendsen) on existing MC data — requiring no new simulations — and refines Tc from 4.602 ± 0.167 (2.0% error) to 4.572 ± 0.069 (1.3% error), a 2.4× precision improvement. This collapses the β CI inflation from 27× to 16.7× (39% reduction). The system crosses the boundary from "knowing when it is wrong" to "fixing itself when it is wrong."

We demonstrate universality of the Tc-dominance finding via a cross-system test: exact Tc → 1.1× inflation, noisy Tc → 2.8× inflation in 2D Ising (a controlled setting, not a distinct universality class). Distributional robustness testing (Gaussian vs. t(df=3) vs. widened Gaussian) shows CI width varies by only 26%, confirming the dominance conclusion is insensitive to the parametric choice.

Introduction

Autonomous scientific discovery promises to accelerate research by replacing human-in-the-loop hypothesis testing with end-to-end computational pipelines [1, 2]. In computational physics, this typically involves parameter estimation under uncertainty — for example, determining critical exponents from Monte Carlo data. State-of-the-art studies achieve six-digit precision using lattices up to L = 1024 [3], but require expert design choices at every stage.

We ask a different question: what can a fully autonomous pipeline achieve on modest resources, and — critically — can it repair its own dominant systematic when detected? This pipeline does not use large language models; it is a deterministic, auditable computational pipeline written in pure Python, with all decisions encoded as explicit rules. Its 20 fully automated phases execute with 7 human-specified pre-run parameters: lattice sizes, temperature range, sweep counts, functional form, quality thresholds, bootstrap count, and success criterion.

1.1 Discovery, estimation, diagnosis, and self-repair

We distinguish four levels of autonomous scientific work:

  • Discovery — selecting the correct functional form (e.g., power law vs. exponential). Addressed via AIC/BIC model comparison across four candidate forms, validated by ROC analysis on 200 synthetic datasets (100 true power law at SNR ∈ [5, 100], 100 non-power-law: polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, stretched-exponential). R² threshold swept [0.5, 0.98]; thresholds calibrated on synthetic data only.
  • Estimation — fitting parameters within the selected form. OLS + IVW, sensitivity analysis across 6 configurations, and a trimming sweep (0%–40%).
  • Diagnosis — quantifying failure modes and identifying which error terms dominate. A unified engine inspects residual diagnostics, dual CIs, model selection, SNR, and reproducibility, producing a structured health report with severity levels and actionable recommendations.
  • Self-repair (new in v11) — automatically executing the top-priority recommendation. When diagnosis detects SEVERE Tc inflation, the pipeline triggers single-histogram reweighting on existing data, refines Tc, re-estimates exponents, and re-runs dual CIs — all without human intervention or new MC simulations.

Most autonomous systems in physics operate at levels 1–2 and stop. We demonstrate that levels 3–4 can be implemented as first-class pipeline components that output actionable results, not just uncertainty bars. This positions the work in the broader "agentic science" landscape [2], distinguished by its fully auditable, deterministic, non-LLM design.

4.3 Signal-to-noise bridge

The SNR is defined as mean(|signal|) / std(residuals) from the power-law fit, providing an empirical estimate of signal quality. M(t) SNR ≈ 31 falls in the SNR = 20–50 regime where synthetic calibration predicts 5–19% fitting error — consistent with the observed 15.4% raw error. χ(t) SNR ≈ 2 is below all calibration levels, explaining the poor γ recovery independently of fitting methodology.

4.4 Corrections-to-scaling

Standard FSS: β/ν = 0.779 (50%). Adding M(Tc, L) = L−β/ν(a₀ + a₁L−ω) with ω = 0.83 [4]: Wolff improves to β/ν = 0.706 (36.2%, −3.8 pp) with a₁/a₀ = 0.095 (moderate corrections). Metropolis overshoots to β/ν = 1.193 (130%) with a₁/a₀ = 1.54 — the correction term exceeds the leading term, signaling instability at L ≤ 12.

General Principle: Tc Dominance

We now have convergent evidence from four independent sources:

  1. 3D Ising: 27× β CI inflation at 2% Tc error (Section 5).
  2. 2D Ising (controlled): exact Tc → 1.1× inflation; noisy Tc (2% error) → 2.8× inflation (Section 7).
  3. Distributional robustness: 26% CI variation across three distributions, all showing massive inflation (Section 5.1).
  4. Self-repair: halving Tc uncertainty via reweighting reduces inflation from 27× to 17× (Section 8).

This leads to a general principle:

Principle (Tc Dominance).

In autonomous critical exponent pipelines operating on finite-size lattices, Tc uncertainty dominates exponent uncertainty whenever (a) the reduced-temperature window is narrow, (b) the exponent is fit near Tc, and (c) Tc is estimated rather than known. The inflation scales with σ(Tc) nonlinearly through the reduced temperature t = |T − Tc|/Tc.

This principle is not specific to our pipeline. Any autonomous system that estimates Tc and then conditions on that estimate for power-law fitting will exhibit this inflation. The scaling Tc(L) − Tc(∞) ~ L−(1/ν+ω) is a standard finite-size scaling result [4, 8]; our contribution is showing that this well-known shift propagates nonlinearly into exponent CIs and dominates the total error budget at modest L. The only ways to suppress it are: (a) improve Tc precision (larger L or better methods), (b) use observables with wider critical windows, or (c) jointly fit Tc and exponents.

Cross-System Generalization

To test whether Tc dominance is general, we apply the identical pipeline — with no code changes — to the 2D Ising model (L ∈ {8, 12, 16, 24, 32}). The 2D system serves as a controlled setting for manipulating Tc uncertainty (exact Tc = 2/ln(1+√2) is known analytically), not as a probe of a distinct universality class. Probing a genuinely different class (e.g. 3D XY or Heisenberg) is planned for v12.

Closed-Loop Self-Repair

The v11 breakthrough: the pipeline detects its own dominant systematic and automatically fixes it. The self-repair loop proceeds as follows:

  1. Trigger: Self-diagnosis reports SEVERE β CI inflation (27×) and ADEQUATE Tc precision (2.0%). The top recommendation is: "extend to L ≥ 24 or add histogram reweighting."
  2. Method: Single-histogram reweighting [7, 8] on the MC data already collected. For each temperature T₀ where samples {Ei, mi} exist, compute ⟨O⟩T = Σ Oiwi / Σ wi where wi = exp(−Δβ · Ei · N) and Δβ = 1/T − 1/T₀. The effective sample size neff = (Σwi)² / Σwi² degrades as |T − T₀| grows; we mitigate this by always reweighting from the closest measured temperature.
  3. Tc refinement: Compute continuous U₄(T) curves at each L via reweighting, find all pairwise crossings, take median with MAD-based uncertainty.
  4. Re-estimation: Refit all exponents with refined Tc, re-run dual CIs.
  5. Verify: Post-repair self-diagnosis checks whether the issue improved.

Limitations and Path Forward

12.1 Why γ is hard at small L

Three factors conspire: (1) χ(t) near Tc has SNR ≈ 2 — fluctuations are largest where the power law should hold; (2) the power-law regime spans only ~3× in reduced temperature at L = 12; (3) even after IVW, χ residuals fail Shapiro–Wilk. The γ roadmap: L ≥ 32 to widen the regime, histogram reweighting for continuous χ(T) curves, corrections-to-scaling in the FSS fit.

12.4 Methodological next steps

  1. Code and data DOI: The pipeline is pure Python with no external dependencies beyond numpy and scipy. Packaging for Zenodo is the primary gate to submission — the code repository and all raw MC data will be archived with a persistent DOI.
  2. Bayesian Tc module: Replace the Gaussian Tc assumption with a full posterior P(Tc | data) via Binder crossing likelihoods. The posterior would define Tc ∝ ∏L₁,L₂ P(U₄(L₁)(T) = U₄(L₂)(T)), marginalised as a nuisance parameter when fitting exponents.
  3. Second universality class: Running on 3D XY or Heisenberg (different exponents, same pipeline) would change "autonomous rediscovery" to "autonomous discovery that transfers to genuinely new physics."
  4. Multi-histogram reweighting: Extending from single-histogram (used in v11) to WHAM-style multi-histogram would combine data across all 30 temperatures simultaneously, further tightening Tc.

Conclusions

We have presented the first fully autonomous, self-correcting pipeline for critical exponent discovery. The system operates at four levels — discovery, estimation, diagnosis, and repair — and demonstrates on two Ising systems that Tc uncertainty dominates exponent uncertainty at modest lattice sizes. This is a general principle, not a pipeline-specific artifact.

The v11 breakthrough is closed-loop self-repair: the self-diagnosis engine detects SEVERE Tc inflation, autonomously triggers Ferrenberg–Swendsen single-histogram reweighting on stored MC data, and refines Tc from 2.0% to 1.3% error — a 2.4× precision improvement at zero additional MC cost. This collapses the β CI inflation from 27× to 17×, and the γ inflation from 2.2× to 1.7×.

The system crosses the boundary from "knows when it is wrong" to "fixes itself when it is wrong." We claim a methodological breakthrough in autonomous, self-correcting Monte Carlo pipelines, not in the numerical precision of 3D Ising exponents, which remains orders of magnitude behind specialised studies [3, 4]. The novelty lies in how the pipeline audits, attributes, and then reduces its own uncertainty at modest L. The path forward is clear and costed: L = 32 (~52 min realistic) would contract β's CI by 10.7×. Multi-histogram reweighting + Bayesian Tc inference would eliminate the dominant systematic entirely. The pipeline requires no architectural changes for either improvement.
