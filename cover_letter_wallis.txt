Please consider the manuscript "A One-Parameter Family of Wallis-Type Polynomial Continued Fractions: Discovery and Formal Verification" for publication in the Journal of Symbolic Computation. The paper proves that the continued fractions

  S^(m) = 1 + K_{n>=1} [-n(2n-(2m+1)) / (3n+1)]

admit the closed form 2^(2m+1)/(pi * binom(2m,m)) for every m in N, yielding a new one-parameter Wallis-type family. In addition, the manuscript gives a zero-sorry Lean 4 verification of the recurrence transport lemma, the Wallis base case, and the inductive limit step, so the main theorem is backed by a machine-checked proof chain rather than by numerical evidence alone.

We believe the paper is particularly well suited to the Journal of Symbolic Computation because its main methodological contribution is a reusable Lean-oriented proof pattern. The key intertwining lemma transports solutions between adjacent recurrence parameters and turns the full family proof into a symbolic computation problem plus a formal limit step. This combination of automated discovery, exact recurrence manipulation, and formal certification aligns closely with the journal's symbolic-computation mission.
