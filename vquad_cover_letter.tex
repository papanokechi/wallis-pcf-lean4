\opening{Dear Editors,}

We submit the manuscript ``A new transcendental constant from a
quadratic continued fraction: Exclusion from classical
special-function families'' for consideration in
\textit{Experimental Mathematics}.

The paper introduces and studies the constant
$V_{\mathrm{quad}} = 1 + K_{n \ge 1} \frac{1}{3n^2 + n + 1}
\approx 1.19737$, a generalized continued fraction with quadratic
partial denominators and discriminant $\Delta = -11$.  The main
contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item A proof that $V_{\mathrm{quad}}$ is irrational with optimal
  irrationality measure $\mu = 2$, via a symplectic Wronskian
  identity for the convergent sequence.

\item A comprehensive exclusion theorem showing that
  $V_{\mathrm{quad}}$ is not a rational linear combination
  (coefficient bound $|c_i| \le 10{,}000$) of values from $16$
  classical special-function families, established by over
  $20{,}000$ individual PSLQ searches at precision levels up
  to $2{,}050$ digits.  The tested families include hypergeometric
  functions, Airy, Bessel, and parabolic cylinder functions,
  complete elliptic integrals, Meijer $G$- and Lommel functions,
  $L$-values and periods of the conductor-$11$ elliptic curve
  $E_{11a1}$, Eisenstein series, Dedekind eta products, and
  Ramanujan's mock theta functions.

\item Evidence that the generating function of the convergent
  denominators has an irregular singular point, placing
  $V_{\mathrm{quad}}$ outside the D-finite (holonomic) class.
\end{enumerate}

We also report a degree-$2$ PSLQ false-positive mechanism
affecting rational CF values (Section~5.1) and two additional
unidentified irrational constants from higher-order recurrences
(Section~4.5).

A self-contained Python script reproducing all core computations
is included as supplementary material.

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere.

\closing{Sincerely,}
