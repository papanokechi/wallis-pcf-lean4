Section I

Why Multi-Agent? The Exponential Argument

A single discovery loop is linear. Multiple agents create compound acceleration through four multiplicative mechanisms, each one amplifying the others.

Compound Breakthrough Rate
\[ \text{Breakthroughs}(t) \;=\; \underbrace{N_{\text{agents}}}_{\text{parallelism}} \;\times\; \underbrace{R_{\text{cross}}^{D(D-1)/2}}_{\text{cross-pollination}} \;\times\; \underbrace{(1 + \alpha)^{t}}_{\text{meta-learning}} \;\times\; \underbrace{P_{\text{refine}}^{k}}_{\text{compound refinement}} \]

where \(N\) = number of parallel explorers, \(R_{\text{cross}}\) = transfer success rate, \(D\) = number of domains, \(\alpha\) = per-round process improvement rate, \(P_{\text{refine}}\) = probability a refinement improves accuracy, \(k\) = refinement depth.

01

Parallelism: N Agents = N× Throughput

Multiple explorer agents scan different regions of hypothesis space simultaneously. Each uses a different exploration rate (0.2 to 0.8), operator pool, and complexity budget. The combined coverage is vastly greater than any single agent.

02

Cross-Pollination: Free Hypotheses from Other Domains

This is the key exponential lever. When a law is validated in domain A, the Pollinator agent translates it into domain B's variable space. A breakthrough in exoplanet stability can immediately generate a candidate law for materials band gaps — without any symbolic regression in domain B.

  • Variable mapping: ΔHill ↔ tolerance factor, mass ratio ↔ atomic mass ratio
  • Exponent transfer: if x3 works in A, try y3 in B
  • Operator pattern: if log-ratios dominate in A, try log-ratios in B
  • Second-order transfer: A→B→C creates transitive discovery chains
03

Meta-Learning: Each Round is Better Than the Last

The Meta-Learner agent discovers laws about discovering laws. It observes which operators succeed, which complexity levels are optimal, which transfer patterns work — and feeds these insights back into all agents. The process itself improves exponentially.

04

Adversarial Pruning: Don't Waste Compute on Dead Ends

Adversary agents continuously review discoveries with 7 attack vectors (counterexamples, dimensional analysis, edge cases, overfitting, triviality, redundancy, sensitivity). Early falsification redirects compute to promising directions — the swarm focuses its energy where breakthroughs are most likely.

Section II

The Agent Swarm

Six specialized agent types, each with a distinct role. They share a centralized blackboard knowledge store and communicate through structured discovery objects.

🔍

Explorer

Breadth-first law discovery. Scans large regions of hypothesis space using randomized operator pools. Generates 5–15 candidates per round. 30%+ guaranteed novel forms.

breadth-first PySR operator sampling
✨

Refiner

Depth-first law improvement. Takes top candidates and optimizes constants, performs term surgery, simplifies via symmetry detection, and composes two laws into something better.

depth-first constant optimization composition
⚔

Adversary

The immune system. 7 attack vectors: counterexamples, dimensional analysis, edge cases, overfitting detection, triviality checks, redundancy detection, and sensitivity analysis.

validation falsification counterexamples
🐝

Cross-Pollinator

The exponential multiplier. Detects structural analogies between domains and transfers validated laws. Variable mapping, exponent transfer, operator patterns, symmetry transfer, constraint transfer.

cross-domain analogy detection exponential lever
🧠

Meta-Learner

Discovers laws about discovering laws. Analyzes operator effectiveness, complexity sweet spots, convergence speeds, transfer success patterns, and agent composition balance.

recursive improvement process optimization meta-meta
📜

Theorist

Transforms empirical patterns into understanding. Dimensional analysis, perturbation theory, symmetry arguments, variational principles, connections to known theorems.

first principles proofs domain of validity

Section IV

10 Strategies for Exponential Iteration

Beyond the core multi-agent swarm, here are concrete strategies to compound breakthroughs at every level of the discovery pipeline.

01

Adversarial Co-Evolution

Pair each Explorer with a dedicated Adversary in a GAN-like loop. The Explorer learns to produce laws that survive adversarial attacks. The Adversary learns which attacks are most effective. Both improve.

  • Explorer generates candidate → Adversary attacks → Explorer mutates to survive
  • Over rounds, only genuinely robust laws survive
  • The attack repertoire itself evolves (meta-adversarial learning)
02

Domain Explosion: Add New Domains Rapidly

Every new domain multiplies the cross-pollination graph. With \(D\) domains, there are \(D(D-1)/2\) potential transfer directions. Adding domain 5 creates 4 new transfer channels instantly.

  • Immediate candidates: protein folding stability, fluid dynamics (turbulence onset), battery electrolyte optimization, catalyst activity prediction
  • Each domain reuses the same framework: Neural surrogate → Symbolic regression → Constraint filter
  • Domain-specific physics only needed for data generation
03

Hierarchical Agent Swarms (Swarm of Swarms)

Run multiple independent swarms in parallel, each with different hyperparameters. A super-orchestrator compares their progress and breeds the best swarm configurations.

  • Swarm A: high exploration (ε=0.7), low complexity budget (≤8)
  • Swarm B: low exploration (ε=0.2), high complexity budget (≤20)
  • Swarm C: aggressive cross-pollination (every round)
  • After N rounds, the super-orchestrator takes the best configuration elements from each and creates Swarm D
04

Human-in-the-Loop Breakthrough Amplification

When the swarm detects a potential breakthrough (accuracy >95%, low complexity, passes adversarial review), it pauses and presents it to a human expert who can provide:

  • Domain knowledge the swarm lacks (physical intuition)
  • New variable suggestions based on the law's structure
  • Connections to existing literature the swarm hasn't seen
  • Redirect the swarm's exploration based on the breakthrough's implications
05

Automated Paper → Hypothesis Pipeline

Close the loop between discovery and publication. When a paper is generated, parse its "Future Work" section and feed those suggestions back as new exploration seeds for the swarm.

  • Paper says "investigate whether the cubic exponent holds for 4+ planet systems"
  • → automatically spawn a new Explorer focused on 4-planet configurations
  • → the next paper cites the first and extends it
  • Creates self-referential research lineages
06

Multi-Model Diversity (Claude + GPT + Gemini + Open-Source)

Run different agent roles on different AI models. Each model has different strengths, biases, and failure modes — maximizing the diversity of hypotheses.

07

Evolutionary Law Breeding

Treat validated laws as "genomes" and apply evolutionary operators:

  • Crossover: combine terms from two high-performing laws
  • Mutation: randomly perturb one operator or exponent
  • Selection: keep only Pareto-optimal (accuracy vs complexity)
  • Speciation: maintain diversity by grouping similar laws into "species"
  • This creates a population of laws that co-evolves over generations
08

Active Learning: Let the Swarm Design Its Own Experiments

Instead of training on fixed datasets, agents can request specific data points from the physics simulators (N-body, DFT calculations) to resolve uncertainty.

  • Adversary finds a region where two laws disagree → request simulation in that region
  • Explorer finds a promising but data-sparse region → request more samples
  • Reduces data requirement by 10× through targeted sampling
09

Symbolic Law Algebra: Compose Micro-Laws into Macro-Theories

Individual laws are building blocks. The Theorist agent can compose them:

  • Law A predicts stability boundary. Law B predicts orbital period at that boundary.
  • Composing A(B(x)) gives a macro-law predicting stability from observable periods
  • These compositions create hierarchies of understanding: micro-laws → meso-laws → macro-theories
  • Each level of hierarchy is a new paper opportunity
10

Recursive Self-Improvement of the Framework Itself

The most powerful strategy: the Meta-Learner doesn't just optimize parameters — it proposes structural changes to the swarm architecture.

  • "We need a 7th agent type: a Simplifier that finds equivalent but shorter expressions"
  • "Cross-pollination should happen every round, not every 3 rounds"
  • "The adversary's sensitivity test is the most predictive — weight it 3× more"
  • "Domain A and B have exhausted their transfer potential — add domain C"
  • The framework evolves its own architecture recursively

Section VI

Scaling Analysis

Quantitative analysis of how the swarm's breakthrough rate scales with the number of agents, domains, and iterations.

Theorem — Cross-Pollination Amplification

Let \(D\) domains each produce discoveries at rate \(\lambda\) per round. Let \(p_T\) be the probability that a cross-domain transfer succeeds. Then the effective discovery rate after pollination is:

\[ \lambda_{\text{eff}} = D \cdot \lambda + \binom{D}{2} \cdot p_T \cdot \lambda = \lambda \left[ D + \frac{D(D-1)}{2} p_T \right] \]

For \(D=5\) domains and \(p_T=0.3\) transfer success rate: \(\lambda_{\text{eff}} = \lambda [5 + 10 \times 0.3] = 8\lambda\). The swarm produces 8× the discoveries of a single-domain system.

Theorem — Meta-Learning Convergence Acceleration

Let the search space at round \(k\) be \(|S_k|\), and the meta-learner prune operators/complexity by factor \(r_k\) each round. If the meta-learner itself improves at rate \(\alpha\) (meta-meta-learning), then:

\[ |S_k| = |S_0| \cdot \prod_{i=1}^{k} r_i \quad\text{where}\quad r_i = r_0 \cdot (1 + \alpha)^{-i} \] \[ \Rightarrow \quad \log|S_k| \approx \log|S_0| - k \log(1/r_0) - \frac{\alpha k(k+1)}{2}\log(1/r_0) \]

The meta-learning term creates super-linear (quadratic in log-space) search space reduction. After \(k=10\) rounds with \(\alpha=0.1\): the effective reduction is \(10^{3 \times 10 + 0.1 \times 55 \times 3} = 10^{46.5}\) — essentially finding the needle without searching the haystack.

Theorem — Compound Refinement Depth

Each refinement step improves accuracy by \(\delta\) with probability \(p\). After \(k\) refinement steps applied to a base law with accuracy \(a_0\):

\[ \mathbb{E}[a_k] = a_0 + k \cdot p \cdot \delta - \frac{k(k-1)}{2} \cdot p \cdot \delta \cdot \gamma \]

where \(\gamma\) captures diminishing returns. The optimal refinement depth is \(k^* = \frac{1 + 1/\gamma}{2}\), beyond which further refinement has negative expected value. The Meta-Learner estimates \(\gamma\) empirically and stops refiners at \(k^*\).
