Ratio Universality for Meinardus-Class Partition Functions: A Complete Asymptotic Expansion with Numerical Verification Across Six Families

Autonomous Discovery Relay Chain — Rounds 2–10

Multi-Agent Conjecture Discovery Framework — Yokohama, Japan

Abstract

We establish a universal asymptotic expansion for the consecutive ratio \(R_m = f(m)/f(m-1)\) of sequences with Meinardus-type growth \(f(n) \sim C\, n^{\kappa}\, e^{c\sqrt{n}}\). The expansion takes the form \[ R_m = 1 + \frac{c}{2\sqrt{m}} + \frac{L}{m} + \frac{\alpha}{m^{3/2}} + O(m^{-2}), \] where \(L = c^2/8 + \kappa\) depends only on the growth constant \(c\) and the Meinardus exponent \(\kappa\). We verify this formula numerically for six families: \(k\)-colored partitions with \(k = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5\) and overpartitions, achieving agreement at the \(0.01\%\)–\(0.1\%\) level. For \(k=1\) (standard partitions), we derive the complete 4-term expansion with all coefficients in exact closed form, verified to a residual of \(4.83 \times 10^{-12}\) at \(m = 4000\). The third-order coefficient \(\alpha\) is determined for \(k = 1, 2, 3\) and overpartitions via Richardson extrapolation, revealing that the sub-leading Meinardus correction \(A_1\) depends non-trivially on the family. We correct a persistent identity error in the relay chain: the OEIS sequence A002865 satisfies \(a(n) = p(n) - p(n-1)\), not \(a(n) = p(n-2)\).

1. Introduction

The partition function \(p(n)\) — counting the number of ways to write \(n\) as a sum of positive integers — is among the most studied objects in combinatorics and number theory. Hardy and Ramanujan's celebrated 1918 result established the asymptotic growth \(p(n) \sim \frac{1}{4n\sqrt{3}} \exp(\pi\sqrt{2n/3})\), subsequently refined by Rademacher's exact convergent series.

Far less studied is the consecutive ratio \(R_m = p(m)/p(m-1)\), which encodes the relative growth rate of the partition function. This ratio converges to 1 as \(m \to \infty\), but how it converges — the precise structure of the correction terms — reveals deep connections between the combinatorial generating function and the analytic number theory of the Rademacher expansion.

In this paper, we derive and verify a universal asymptotic expansion for \(R_m\) that applies not just to ordinary partitions, but to any sequence with Meinardus-type asymptotics. The second-order coefficient \(L\) takes the remarkably simple form

\[ L = \frac{c^2}{8} + \kappa, \]

where \(c\) is the growth constant and \(\kappa\) is the Meinardus prefactor exponent. This formula is verified across six distinct combinatorial families, establishing it as a genuinely universal phenomenon.

The work emerged from a multi-agent relay chain — an adversarial protocol where successive agents propose, verify, and refute conjectures about OEIS sequence A002865. Over 10 rounds, the relay corrected a persistent identity error, derived the expansion term by term, and extended it to multiple families. The relay's failure archive (§8) documents the epistemic process, including three independent erroneous identities that were propagated and subsequently killed.

2. Background

2.1 Meinardus Asymptotics

Consider an infinite product of the form \[ F(q) = \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1 - q^m)^{-a_m} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} f(n)\, q^n, \] where \(a_m \geq 0\) are non-negative integers with associated Dirichlet series \(D(s) = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} a_m\, m^{-s}\). The Meinardus theorem (1954) asserts that, under mild analytic conditions on \(D(s)\),

\begin{equation} f(n) \sim C\, n^{\kappa}\, e^{c\sqrt{n}} \qquad (n \to \infty), \label{eq:meinardus} \end{equation}

where the growth constant and prefactor exponent are determined by \(D(s)\):

\[ c = \sqrt{2A \cdot \tfrac{\pi^2}{3}}, \qquad \kappa = \frac{D(0) - \frac{3}{2}}{2}, \]

with \(A = \text{Res}_{s=1} D(s)\) the residue at the rightmost pole of \(D(s)\).

2.2 \(k\)-Colored Partitions

For \(k\)-colored partitions, where each integer has \(k\) distinguishable colors, the generating function is \[ F_k(q) = \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1 - q^m)^{-k}, \] with \(D(s) = k\, \zeta(s)\). The Meinardus parameters are:

2.3 Overpartitions

Overpartitions — partitions where the first occurrence of each part may be overlined — have generating function \[ \bar{F}(q) = \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{1 + q^m}{1 - q^m} = \prod_{m=1}^{\infty} (1-q^m)^{-a_m}, \] where \(a_m = 2\) for odd \(m\) and \(a_m = 1\) for even \(m\). The Dirichlet series is \(D(s) = \zeta(s)(2 - 2^{-s})\) with residue \(A = 3/2\), giving \(c = \pi\) and \(\kappa = -1\).

3. Main Result: Ratio Universality

Theorem 1 (Ratio Universality for Meinardus-Class Sequences)

Let \(\{f(n)\}\) be a sequence with Meinardus-type asymptotics \[ f(n) \sim C\, n^{\kappa}\, e^{c\sqrt{n}} \bigl(1 + A_1 n^{-1/2} + A_2 n^{-1} + \cdots\bigr) \] where \(c > 0\) and \(\kappa \in \mathbb{R}\). Then the consecutive ratio satisfies

\begin{equation} R_m \;=\; \frac{f(m)}{f(m-1)} \;=\; 1 + \frac{c}{2\sqrt{m}} + \frac{L}{m} + \frac{\alpha}{m^{3/2}} + O(m^{-2}), \label{eq:main} \end{equation}

where

\begin{align} L &= \frac{c^2}{8} + \kappa, \label{eq:L} \\[6pt] \alpha &= \frac{c(c^2 + 6)}{48} + \frac{c\kappa}{2} - \frac{A_1}{2}. \label{eq:alpha} \end{align}

Proof sketch. The ratio factors as \(R_m = E_m \cdot P_m \cdot S_m\) where:

Exponential factor: \(E_m = \exp(c\sqrt{m} - c\sqrt{m-1})\). Setting \(u = 1/m\) and expanding \(\sqrt{1-u}\): \[ c(\sqrt{m} - \sqrt{m-1}) = \frac{c}{2\sqrt{m}} + \frac{c}{8m^{3/2}} + O(m^{-5/2}). \] Exponentiating: \[ E_m = 1 + \frac{c}{2\sqrt{m}} + \frac{c^2}{8m} + \frac{c(c^2+6)}{48\, m^{3/2}} + O(m^{-2}). \]

Power-law factor: \(P_m = (m/(m-1))^{\kappa} = (1 - 1/m)^{-\kappa}\): \[ P_m = 1 + \frac{\kappa}{m} + O(m^{-2}). \] Note \(P_m\) contributes no term at \(m^{-1/2}\) or \(m^{-3/2}\) — only integer powers.

Sub-leading factor: \(S_m = (1 + A_1/\sqrt{m} + \cdots)/(1 + A_1/\sqrt{m-1} + \cdots)\). Expanding \(1/\sqrt{m-1} = m^{-1/2}(1 + 1/(2m) + \cdots)\): \[ S_m = 1 - \frac{A_1}{2m^{3/2}} + O(m^{-2}). \]

Multiplying \(E_m \cdot P_m \cdot S_m\) and collecting terms at each order:

  • \(O(m^{-1/2})\): \(c/2\) — from \(E_m\) alone.
  • \(O(m^{-1})\): \(c^2/8 + \kappa = L\) — cross-term \(E_m \times P_m\).
  • \(O(m^{-3/2})\): \(c(c^2+6)/48 + c\kappa/2 - A_1/2 = \alpha\) — all three factors contribute.  \(\square\)

4. Exact 4-Term Expansion for \(k = 1\)

For the standard partition function, the Rademacher expansion provides exact sub-leading corrections. Working from the \(k=1\) term of the Rademacher series and the shift \(\mu = n - 1/24\):

\[ p(n) \sim \frac{1}{4n\sqrt{3}}\, e^{c_1\sqrt{n}} \left(1 + \frac{A_1}{\sqrt{n}} + \cdots\right) \]

where \(A_1 = -c_1/48 - 1/c_1\), arising from two sources: the \(\mu = n - 1/24\) shift (contributing \(-c_1/48\)) and the Rademacher derivative structure (contributing \(-1/c_1\)).

This yields the exact coefficients (derived in Round 7 of the relay):

where \(c_1 = \pi\sqrt{2/3}\), so \(c_1^2/8 - 1 = \pi^2/12 - 1 \approx -0.17753\)

6. The Third-Order Coefficient \(\alpha_k\)

6.1 Structure

By \eqref{eq:alpha}, the third-order coefficient is \[ \alpha = \frac{c(c^2+6)}{48} + \frac{c\kappa}{2} - \frac{A_1}{2}, \] where \(A_1\) is the coefficient of \(n^{-1/2}\) in the sub-leading correction of the Meinardus expansion. This correction is family-specific: it depends on the full structure of the Dirichlet series \(D(s)\), not just on \(c\) and \(\kappa\).

6.2 Extraction via Richardson Extrapolation

We extract \(\alpha_k\) numerically by defining \[ D_m = \sqrt{m}\,(C_m - L) \] and applying Richardson extrapolation to cancel the \(O(m^{-1/2})\) correction in \(D_m\). Using pairs \((m_1, 4m_1)\):

Key Finding: \(A_1\) is Family-Specific

The simple formula \(A_1 = -c/48 + \kappa/c\) — derived from the \(k=1\) Rademacher expansion — does not generalize to other families. For \(k \geq 2\), the sub-leading Meinardus correction involves additional terms from the Dirichlet series \(D(s)\) evaluated at negative integers, Bernoulli-type contributions, and the refined Wright circle method. Determining \(A_1^{(k)}\) in closed form for general \(k\) is the natural next frontier.

7. Extension to Overpartitions

Overpartitions provide a critical test of the general theorem because they lie outside the \(k\)-colored family. Their Meinardus parameters (\(c = \pi\), \(\kappa = -1\)) are derived from the Dirichlet series \(D(s) = \zeta(s)(2 - 2^{-s})\), which has a qualitatively different structure from \(k\,\zeta(s)\).

The predicted \(L = \pi^2/8 - 1 \approx 0.23370\) matches the extrapolated value \(0.23393\) to 0.097%, confirming that the \(L = c^2/8 + \kappa\) formula is truly universal — it depends only on the leading Meinardus parameters, not on the detailed structure of \(D(s)\).

8. Identity Correction and Epistemic Hygiene

8.1 The A002865 Identity

Critical Correction (Round 10)

OEIS A002865 ("partitions of \(n\) not containing 1 as a part") satisfies \[ a(n) = p(n) - p(n-1) \qquad (n \geq 1), \] not \(a(n) = p(n-2)\) as claimed in Rounds 2–9. The earlier "verification" used a corrupted reference array (values incorrect from \(n = 21\) onward). The correct identity was confirmed against 51 OEIS-verified terms and by direct enumeration.

Impact: The ratio \(R_m = p(m)/p(m-1)\) satisfies \(R_m = 1 + a(m)/p(m-1)\), so the expansion directly characterizes the growth of A002865 relative to the partition function. All mathematical results on \(R_m\) remain valid because they depend on \(p(m)\), not on the identity.

9.2 The Hierarchy of Universality

Our results reveal a hierarchy of universality for the ratio expansion:

  1. Fully universal (depends on \(c, \kappa\) only): The leading correction \(c/(2\sqrt{m})\) and the second-order coefficient \(L = c^2/8 + \kappa\). These are determined entirely by the Meinardus parameters.
  2. Structurally universal (format fixed, coefficients family-specific): The third-order coefficient \(\alpha\), which has the form \(c(c^2+6)/48 + c\kappa/2 - A_1/2\) for any Meinardus sequence, but where \(A_1\) depends on the specific Dirichlet series.
  3. Fully determined for \(k=1\): The complete 4-term expansion with all coefficients in exact closed form, verified to \(10^{-12}\) precision.

9.3 Open Problems

Problem 1. Determine \(A_1^{(k)}\) in closed form for \(k\)-colored partitions with \(k \geq 2\). This requires the sub-leading correction in the Wright circle method / refined Meinardus expansion, tracking how \(D(s)\) at negative integers contributes to the \(n^{-1/2}\) term.

Problem 2. Derive the fourth-order coefficient \(\beta_k\) (coefficient of \(m^{-2}\)) for general \(k\). For \(k=1\), we have the exact form involving \(\pi^6\), \(\pi^4\), etc. The general case involves both \(A_1\) and a second correction \(A_2\).

Problem 3. Extend to families with different pole structures. Plane partitions, for instance, have \(f(n) \sim C\, n^{\kappa}\, e^{c\, n^{1/3}}\) (cube-root growth). Does a ratio universality hold with \(c/(3n^{2/3})\) replacing \(c/(2\sqrt{n})\)?

Problem 4. Prove the general theorem rigorously. Our derivation assumes the Meinardus expansion can be differentiated term-by-term in the ratio. A rigorous proof would need to control the error terms uniformly.

10. Computational Methods

10.1 Partition Function Computation

The standard partition function \(p(n)\) is computed via Euler's pentagonal number recurrence with memoization, giving exact integer values up to \(n = 4000\). For \(k\)-colored partitions, we use the recurrence \(n\, p_k(n) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} k\,\sigma(j)\, p_k(n-j)\), where \(\sigma(j)\) is the sum-of-divisors function. Overpartitions use \(n\,\bar{p}(n) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} c(j)\,\bar{p}(n-j)\) with \(c(j) = 2\sum_{d|j,\, j/d\,\text{odd}} d\).

10.2 High-Precision Arithmetic

All ratio computations use mpmath with 60 decimal places. Partition values are computed as exact Python integers, then converted to arbitrary-precision floats for ratio extraction.

10.3 Fitting and Extrapolation

Meinardus parameters are extracted via least-squares regression of \(\log f(n) - c\sqrt{n}\) against \(\{\ln n,\, 1,\, n^{-1/2},\, n^{-1}\}\). The coefficient \(L\) is extrapolated from \(C_m\) data using the linear ansatz \(C_m = L + A/\sqrt{m}\), and \(\alpha\) is extracted from \(D_m = \sqrt{m}(C_m - L)\) via Richardson extrapolation with pairs \((m, 4m)\).
