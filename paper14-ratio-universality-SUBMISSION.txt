Abstract

growth exponent d. We prove this universality via a selection rule : the three-factor decomposition R = E ¬∑P ¬∑S reveals that the sub-leading Meinardus factor S , which encodes
all family-specic Dirichlet series information, is provably silent at order m , with its rst
contribution delayed to m . For square-root growth (d = 1/2), this gives L = c /8 + őļ;
for cube-root growth (d = 2/3), L = 4c /81 + őļ.
